# Islamic Epistemology for AI Practitioners  
## Course Syllabus (6‑Week Intensive)

**Instructor:** Tawhid‑AI Research Collective  
**Format:** Online (self‑paced) + weekly live seminars  
**Prerequisites:** Basic machine learning (neural networks), curiosity about interdisciplinary ethics  

---

### Week 1: Foundations — Tawhid and Unified Knowledge

**Topics:**
- What is Tawhid? — theological, epistemological, cosmological dimensions
- Unified theories in AI (Newell, LeCun) and the problem of fragmentation
- Mapping Tawhid to world model architecture

**Readings:**
- Al‑Ghazali, *Ihya' Ulum al‑Din* (Introduction)
- Choudhury 2019, *Meta‑Science of Tawhid* (Ch. 1–2)
- Newell 1990, *Unified Theories of Cognition* (Preface)

**Assignment:** Design a diagram of a “Tawhid‑inspired” AI architecture that integrates vision, language, and action.

**Guest Lecture:** Prof. Masudul Choudhury (via recording) on “Tawhidi Methodological Worldview”

---

### Week 2: Developmental Learning — The Embryological Blueprint

**Topics:**
- Quranic embryology: nutfah → alaqah → mudghah → izam → lahm → nash’ah
- Staged developmental learning in AI (curriculum learning, progressive growing)
- Translating 40‑day, 80‑day, 120‑day milestones into epoch schedules

**Readings:**
- Saadat 2009 (Human Embryology and the Holy Quran)
- Suparno 2023 (Cosmology & scientific wonders)
- Bengio 2009 (Curriculum learning)

**Assignment:** Implement a 6‑stage curriculum trainer on MNIST or CIFAR‑10 and compare convergence with baseline.

**Lab:** PyTorch code template for staged scheduler

---

### Week 3: Balance (Mizan) — Fairness as Dynamic Equilibrium

**Topics:**
- The concept of mizan in the Quran (55:7–9) and Islamic law
- Algorithmic fairness: statistical parity, equalized odds, predictive parity
- From static constraints to dynamic balance (Mizan‑Fair regularizer)

**Readings:**
- Siddique & Rauf 2025 (AI, Bioethics, and Islamic Law)
- Nikmah 2025 (God‑Conscious AI)
- Habib 2025 (Maqasid AI Ethics)

**Assignment:** Add Mizan regularizer to a fairness‑utility demo (Adult Income dataset) and plot Pareto front shifts.

**Lab:** `mizan_fairness` library tutorial

---

### Week 4: Light and Transparency (Nur)

**Topics:**
- Nur as divine light (24:35) and its epistemological role
- Explainable AI: attention, saliency, LRP, integrated gradients
- Designing “illumination” visualizations that are culturally resonant

**Readings:**
- Ibn al‑Haytham, *Kitab al‑Manazir* (selected passages on light)
- Recent XAI surveys (Ras et al. 2022)

**Assignment:** Create a Nur‑XAI dashboard for a BERT sentiment model; user study with 5 non‑experts.

**Guest Lecture:** Dr. Ayesha Qaisar (StanfordHAI) on “Cross‑Cultural XAI”

---

### Week 5: Governance — Maqasid al‑Sharīʿah for AI Systems

**Topics:**
- The five necessities (nafs, ‘aql, dīn, nasl, māl) and their translation to AI constraints
- Building a Maqasid compliance test suite
- Case studies: AI in healthcare, finance, surveillance

**Readings:**
- Al‑Jayyousi et al. 2022 (Sustainable Development & Tawhid)
- Manneh 2025 (AI in Islamic Finance: Maqasid review)
- Din & Aslam 2025 (AI Ethics via Maqasid)

**Assignment:** Audit an existing AI application (e.g., COMPAS, loan scorer) against the 5 Maqasid categories; write a 2‑page report.

**Lab:** Using the Maqasid‑Bench evaluation harness.

---

### Week 6: Capstone and Future Directions

**Topics:**
- Integrating all principles into a coherent AI system
- Limitations, criticisms, and responses (Edis, Guénon)
- Publishing your work: venues, peer review, interdisciplinary collaboration

**Final Project:**
- Build a prototype that combines at least two principles (Tawhid + Mizan, or Nur + Maqasid)
- Submit a 4‑page conference‑style paper (target: AIES/FAccT)
- Present in virtual symposium (recorded talk + Q&A)

**Reading:** Full draft of *Divine Ontology in Machine Intelligence* (our paper)
